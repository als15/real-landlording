## Getting Started

### How to Log In

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Safari, or Firefox)

## Managing Service Requests

This is where you'll spend most of your time. When a landlord submits a request for help, it appears here.

### Viewing Requests

1. Click **"Requests"** in the left menu
2. You'll see a list of all requests in a table
3. Each row shows:
   - Landlord's name and email
   - What service they need (plumber, electrician, etc.)
   - Property location
   - How urgent it is
   - Current status

### Filtering Requests

To find specific requests:

1. **Filter by Status:** Click the dropdown that says "Filter by status" and choose:
   - **New** - Just submitted, needs attention
   - **Matching** - You're finding vendors for this
   - **Matched** - Vendors have been assigned
   - **Completed** - Job is done
   - **Cancelled** - Request was cancelled

2. **Search:** Type in the search box to find requests by name, email, or address

### Viewing Request Details

1. Find the request in the list
2. Click the **eye icon** (ğŸ‘) on the right side of that row
3. A panel will slide out showing all the details:
   - Landlord contact information
   - Property details
   - Job description
   - Any photos they uploaded
   - Matched vendors (if any)

### Matching Vendors to a Request

This is the most important part of your job - connecting landlords with the right vendors.

**Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1. Open a request (click the eye icon)
2. Review the request details:
   - What service do they need?
   - Where is the property?
   - How urgent is it?
   - Read the job description carefully
3. Click the **"Match"** button
4. A window will open showing available vendors
5. Check the boxes next to vendors you want to match (up to 3)
6. Click **"Send Introductions"**

## How Matching Works (Behind the Scenes)

Understanding what happens when you match vendors helps you do a better job.

### What You See in the Matching Window

When you click "Match," the system shows you vendors that:
- Offer the service the landlord needs (plumber, electrician, etc.)
- Work in the area where the property is located
- Are marked as "Active" in the system

For each vendor, you'll see:
- **Business name** and contact person
- **Services** they offer
- **Service areas** (zip codes where they work)
- **Rating** (stars based on landlord reviews)
- **Badges** showing if they're Licensed, Insured, or have Rental Experience

### Choosing the Right Vendors

**Good matching tips:**

1. **Match the service exactly** - If they need a plumber, pick plumbers (not handymen)

2. **Check the location** - Make sure the vendor serves that zip code

3. **Consider urgency** - For emergencies, pick vendors known for fast response

4. **Look at ratings** - Higher-rated vendors (more stars) have better track records

5. **Pick 2-3 vendors** - Give the landlord options, but not too many choices

### What the Landlord Receives

The landlord gets an email with:
- A friendly introduction
- **Vendor cards** for each matched vendor showing:
  - Business name
  - Contact person's name
  - Phone number
  - Email address
  - Website (if they have one)
  - Badges: Licensed âœ“, Insured âœ“, Rental Experience âœ“
- Their original job description (so they remember what they asked for)
- **Next steps:** Contact vendors, get quotes, schedule work, leave a review

### What Each Vendor Receives

Each vendor gets their own email with:
- Notice that this is a referral from Real Landlording
- **Complete job details:**
  - Type of service needed
  - Property location
  - How urgent it is
  - Budget range (if the landlord provided one)
  - Full job description
- **Landlord's contact information:**
  - Name
  - Email
  - Phone number
- A request to **respond within 24 hours**

### After You Match

Once you send introductions:

1. **Your job is done** (for now) - The landlord and vendors will contact each other directly

2. **The request shows "Matched"** - You can see this in the status column

3. **Check back later** - In a few days, you might follow up to see if the connection worked

### Important Notes

| Remember | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| You can only match **up to 3 vendors** per request | Too many options confuse landlords |
| You **cannot undo** a match once sent | Double-check before clicking send |
| Emails go out **immediately** | Make sure you selected the right vendors |
| Only **Active** vendors appear | If a vendor is missing, check their status |

## Managing Vendors

### Viewing All Vendors

1. Click **"Vendors"** in the left menu
2. You'll see a list of all vendors with:
   - Business name
   - Contact person
   - Services they offer
   - Rating (stars)
   - Status (Active, Inactive, etc.)

### Searching for Vendors

- Use the **search box** to find vendors by name, email, or phone
- Use the **status filter** to show only Active, Inactive, or Pending vendors

### Viewing Vendor Details

1. Find the vendor in the list
2. Click the **eye icon** (ğŸ‘) on the right
3. A panel slides out showing:
   - Contact information
   - Services they offer
   - Areas they serve (zip codes)
   - Whether they're licensed and insured
   - Their rating and reviews
   - Any notes you've added

### Changing a Vendor's Status

1. Open the vendor details (click the eye icon)
2. Find **"Status"** at the top
3. Click the dropdown menu
4. Choose the new status:
   - **Active** - Available for matching
   - **Inactive** - Temporarily unavailable
   - **Pending Review** - Needs to be reviewed
   - **Rejected** - Not approved

### Editing a Vendor

If you need to update a vendor's information:

1. **Option A - From the list:**
   - Find the vendor in the list
   - Click the **pencil icon** (âœï¸) on the right

2. **Option B - From the details panel:**
   - Open the vendor details (eye icon)
   - Click the blue **"Edit"** button in the top right corner

3. A form will open with all the vendor's information
4. Make your changes
5. Click **"Save Changes"**

### Adding a New Vendor

1. Click the blue **"+ Add Vendor"** button (top right of the page)
2. Fill in the form:
   - **Contact Name** - The person's name
   - **Business Name** - Company name
   - **Email** - Their email address
   - **Phone** - Phone number (optional)
   - **Website** - Their website (optional)
   - **Location** - Where they're based
   - **Services** - Click to select what they do (plumbing, electrical, etc.)
   - **Service Areas** - Type zip codes where they work
   - Check boxes for Licensed, Insured, and Rental Experience
3. Click **"Add Vendor"**

When a vendor applies to join the platform, their application appears here.

### Viewing Applications

### Approving an Application

1. Open the application
2. Review their information carefully:
   - Are they licensed?
   - Are they insured?

1. Open the application
2. If they don't meet your standards, click **"Reject"**
3. You may want to add a note explaining why

## Common Tasks - Quick Reference

### "A landlord just submitted a request. What do I do?"

1. Go to **Requests**
2. Find the new request (it will say "New" in the status)
3. Click the eye icon to review it
4. Click **"Match Vendors"** to assign vendors
5. Select 1-3 appropriate vendors
6. Click **"Send Introductions"**

### "A vendor called to update their phone number"

1. Go to **Vendors**
2. Search for their business name
3. Click the pencil icon to edit
4. Update the phone number
5. Click **"Save Changes"**

### "I need to temporarily remove a vendor from matching"

1. Go to **Vendors**
2. Find the vendor
3. Click the eye icon
4. Change their Status to **"Inactive"**

### "A new vendor wants to join"

Tell them to go to **[your website]/vendor/apply** to fill out an application. You'll see it in the Applications section once they submit.

## Tips for Success

1. **Check Requests Daily** - Landlords expect quick responses. Try to match vendors within 24-48 hours.

2. **Keep Vendor Info Updated** - If a vendor tells you new information, update it right away.

3. **Use Status Filters** - They help you focus on what needs attention (like "New" requests).

4. **Read the Full Request** - Look at the job description and any photos before matching vendors.

5. **Match 2-3 Vendors** - Give landlords options, but not too many.
